- **Key Ideas**
  - Kansoy gave a presentation on "AI, ideas & the future of research" framed around what changes, what doesn't, and what's at stake for the practice of science.
  - The slide deck itself was built using Claude Code --- the meta-point being that the medium of communication is now downstream of agentic tools.
  - From the thread responses: the talk emphasized the progression toward **orchestration systems** as a value-add (Brian Kim's framing), suggesting the deck moves beyond single-prompt LLM use into multi-agent / pipeline territory.
  - One reply (Mark Pors) flagged that the *process* of research itself becomes non-linear in the AI era: the state of the literature on a given question can shift meaningfully over weeks, not years.
  - Ethical dimensions were discussed in the talk but not in the slides (per Kansoy's own clarification).

- **Summary**

This source is a brief X thread announcing the public release of Kansoy's deck on AI and the future of research. The thread itself contains little substantive content beyond a pointer to the slides at fatih.ai/airesearch and a few audience reactions. The substance lives in the deck, which (per the responding researchers) covers the progression from chatbot-style use to orchestration systems. The notable meta-point is that Kansoy built the slide infrastructure with Claude Code, making the artifact itself an instance of the trend it documents.

- **Relevance to Economics Research**

Light. This is best treated as a pointer to a presentation in the same conversational orbit as Hall, Kustov, Andrews, Svoronos, and Cunningham --- i.e., researchers publicly thinking through how agentic tools reshape research workflow. It's worth filing under "what people are saying about the orchestration shift" rather than as a standalone argument.
